Reading the table
  • Form-submit rates are nearly identical across states (17.3% Standard vs 16.9% Premium).
  • ChiliPiper booking rates are also nearly identical (0.79% Standard vs 0.77% Premium).
  • Standard-state delivers the volume: 2,883 form submits + 132 ChiliPiper bookings over 90d.
  • Premium-state is small (519 clickers) but converts at the same rate — the audience reaching it is already qualified.
  • Takeaway: rates don't differ by state — what differs is traffic to the surface. Optimizing the Standard-state CTA scales the absolute pipeline; getting more 100K+ users to the Premium-state CTA grows the high-tier audience without per-click rate gains.
  • Caveat: form-submit and ChiliPiper counts are first-touch — actions taken after the DPM click in the same 90d window.
  • "Sales-form" = on-page form_name="prospective" on /sales; "ChiliPiper" = chili_piper_mtg_booked event on /sales.

1 · One module does ~26× the work

  • Right-side card adds a "Request Demo" button only in Premium state.

  • If optimizing this funnel, the DPM is the surface to test.
2 · The customizer is for price-shopping, not sales handoff
  • Contacts dropdown click events: ~33K / 30d (FullStory heatmap, prospect segment).
  • Note: clicks ≠ unique users — typical click-per-user ratio of 1.5–2× implies ~17–22K unique users opening the dropdown.
  • GA4 doesn't instrument this specific control as a labeled user_interaction event, so the number isn't independently verifiable in BQ — directional only.
  • Continue into a /sales click: ~0.7% (using the click-event denominator).
  • Headroom for a more prominent >100K sales CTA — experiment, not confirmed driver.
3 · /pricing/premium-details/ punches above its weight
  • Page size: small (4,343 visitors / 90d).
  • Click-through to /sales: 6.4% — vs. ~1.4% on /pricing/marketing/.
  • Action: investigate who lands there; route more high-intent traffic to it.
4 · ChiliPiper bookings are the high-intent /sales conversion
  • Two lead-capture mechanisms on /sales: "Request a Demo" → ChiliPiper widget, and the on-page sales lead form (routes to sales team).
  • ChiliPiper meetings booked: 354 unique prospects / 90d (~118/mo) — picked a calendar slot, highest-intent action.

Appendix · form_start / form_submit definitions & sales-form data

What GA4 actually counts

  • form_start — fires the first time a user interacts with any field in a form during a session (GA4 enhanced measurement). Once per form per session. It is not a "form view" — the user has to type, click, or focus into a field.
  • form_submit — fires when the browser's native submit event fires on the form. Triggered by clicking the submit button or hitting Enter. Doesn't guarantee server-side success or that the lead reached the sales team — only that the browser tried to submit.

The two lead-capture mechanisms on /sales

  1. "Request a Demo" CTA → ChiliPiper widget. Opens an embedded calendar; the user picks a slot. Tracked via the custom event chili_piper_mtg_booked. This is the high-intent action surfaced as the primary KPI above (354 unique prospects / 90d).
  2. On-page sales lead form. A standard HTML form (form_id="contactForm" / form_name="prospective") that submits to the sales team for follow-up. Tracked via GA4's form_start / form_submit.

Caveats

  • The 86% start→submit rate is suspiciously high and almost certainly an instrumentation artifact. Most likely cause: browser autofill bypasses the user-interaction trigger that fires form_start, so submits are counted without a corresponding start. Don't trust the ratio at face value — treat form_start on this form as under-counted.
  • form_submit ≠ MQL. No MOps qualification has been applied. As a back-of-envelope: 5,264 submits / 90d ≈ 1,750/mo. At ~40% MOps qualification and historical 10–20% MQL→Opp conversion, that's roughly 70–140 sales opps/mo from the on-page form — but the authoritative number lives in Marketo / Salesforce, not GA4.
  • The KPI card showed ChiliPiper bookings (354) rather than form submits because ChiliPiper bookings are a calendar commitment from the prospect — a higher-confidence signal of sales intent than a form submit.
